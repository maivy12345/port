Healthcare Platform

Client Medpro Healthcare
My Role Lead UX / UI Designer
Deliverables Research · Wireframes · UI System · Prototype
01

Overview

Client Medpro Healthcare
Product Healthcare booking platform - Web & Mobile
Channels Patient portal · Clinic staff app · Admin dashboard

Medpro needed a full redesign of their patient-facing platform to reduce booking friction, increase conversion at key decision points, and unify three disconnected user roles into one coherent product experience.

02

Problem & Context

Patients and medical staff struggled with a fragmented system that made it hard to find doctors, book appointments, and track health records. The interface lacked hierarchy, leading to high drop-off rates at key decision points.

We needed to redesign the core flows from the ground up - creating a unified experience that served three distinct user groups: patients, clinic staff, and administrators.

  • Information overload on the doctor discovery screen
  • Unclear booking status & confirmation flow
  • No unified view for appointment history across clinics
  • Three separate codebases with inconsistent design patterns
03

Design Goals

After synthesising research findings with the product team, we aligned on four measurable design goals:

✦

Reduce booking friction

Cut the steps-to-appointment from 7 to 3 by redesigning the core booking flow.

✦

Unified design system

One shared component library across patient, staff, and admin apps.

✦

Clear information hierarchy

Restructure IA so key actions are reachable within 2 taps from any screen.

✦

Accessible for all ages

WCAG AA compliance - target users span 18-70+ years, many with low digital literacy.

04

User Personas

We identified three primary user archetypes from the research data:

P

The Patient

Primary user · Age 25-60

Wants to quickly find a trusted doctor, book without confusion, and receive clear confirmation. Often anxious; needs reassurance at every step.

  • Too many irrelevant doctors in search results
  • Confusing cancellation & reschedule flow
S

Clinic Staff

Secondary user · Daily power user

Manages multiple appointments, patient check-ins, and records updates throughout the day. Values speed and keyboard-first workflows.

  • No quick-view for daily schedule at a glance
  • Data entry duplicated across two screens
A

Admin

Tertiary user · Oversight role

Needs bird's-eye visibility of clinic performance, booking rates, and staff activity. Works in desktop only; reviews reports weekly.

  • Metrics buried 4 levels deep in navigation
  • No export / share function for reports
05

Process & Decisions

The process was iterative and research-anchored, moving from broad discovery to focused execution over 4 months.

01

Discovery & mapping

User interviews, competitive audit, and analytics review to identify opportunity areas.

02

Information architecture

Card-sorting workshops. Reduced primary navigation from 9 items to 4 clear task-based categories.

03

Wireframing & concept

Lo-fi wireframes for key flows, validated with quick guerrilla tests before high-fidelity.

04

Design system & UI

Scalable component library - tokens, spacing, accessibility, multi-role theming.

05

Prototype & handoff

Interactive prototype tested via Maze. Annotated specs delivered in Figma Dev Mode.

06

Key Screens

Selected screens from the final design system, covering the core patient booking journey and staff dashboard.

07

Results

After launch, the redesigned platform was measured against baseline metrics over a 6-week period:

+42% Booking completion rate
-3.2min Average time to find a doctor
4.8/5 Usability score from Maze testing
3x Faster staff onboarding
08

Reflection

Designing for healthcare means designing for high-stakes moments. Clear, calm UI isn't just an aesthetic choice - it's a functional requirement when users are stressed or unwell.

The biggest shift was moving from "feature parity" to "task clarity." Cutting features that users didn't need freed up space for the flows that actually mattered.

If I were to do this again, I'd push for earlier engineering involvement during IA restructuring - some navigation decisions we made in week 3 had to be revisited after a tech constraints review in week 8.
